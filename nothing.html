Hall of Science

What are Sex-Linked Traits?

Sex-linked traits are characteristics determined by genes located on the sex chromosomes (X and Y). Because females have two X chromosomes (XX) and males have one X and one Y (XY), these traits appear differently in males and females.

Key Concepts:

  • X-Linked Recessive: Most common (e.g., Hemophilia, Color Blindness). Affects males more often because they only have one X chromosome.
  • Carrier: A female who has one gene for the disorder but doesn't show symptoms (XHXh).

Common Disorders

Hemophilia

"The Royal Disease." A disorder where blood doesn't clot normally. Minor injuries can be dangerous.

Color Blindness

Difficulty distinguishing certain colors, usually red and green. It is not blindness, but a different perception.

Literary Wing

"Glass Bones, Paper Skin"

October 14th | Dear Diary,

They asked me to play basketball today during P.E. I did what I always do—I pretended I forgot my shoes. It’s easier to be the "forgetful kid" than the "fragile kid."

How do you explain to a room full of 15-year-olds that a simple trip, a stray elbow, or a hard fall doesn't just mean a bruise for me? It means a hospital trip. It means factor VIII injections. It means pain that throbs deep in the joints, screaming silently while everyone else is laughing.

Mom calls it my "condition." The doctors call it "Hemophilia A." I call it the wall between me and them.

But yesterday, I found something different. I sat on the bleachers, sketching the way the players moved. The fluidity. The chaos. When I showed it to Mr. Santos, he didn't look at me with that pitying 'sick kid' look. He looked at the drawing and said, "You see things they don't have time to see."

Maybe my genes are broken. Or maybe, just maybe, being forced to sit on the sidelines has taught me how to truly watch.

- Leo

Reflection: This piece highlights the social isolation and hidden strengths of living with a genetic disorder.

Bulwagan ng Pagpapahalaga

ANG PAMILYA AY SANDIGAN

"Sa anumang karamdaman, pamilya ang kanlungan."

Pag-aaruga (Care)

Accepting a family member with a genetic disorder means providing not just medical care, but emotional safety. It is the Filipino way of nurturing the vulnerable.

Pagtutulungan (Cooperation)

Genetic disorders can be expensive and exhausting. A united family shares the burden, proving that no one fights alone.

Kapayapaan (Peace)

When we accept differences in our own homes, we learn tolerance. Families that embrace disability become promoters of peace in the community.

Project Reflection

This gallery demonstrates that genetics is not just about X and Y chromosomes. It is about human lives, the stories we tell (Literature), and the values that hold us together (Filipino). Science explains the "how", but our culture and humanity explain the "why" we care.
